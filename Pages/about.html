About

It serves the crucial role of regulating the transfer of power from the engine to the transmission and, ultimately, to the wheels. Positioned between the engine's flywheel and the transmission's pressure plate, the clutch disc plays a central role. It features friction material on both sides and is responsible for engaging and disengaging the engine's power. When you press the clutch pedal inside the car, it initiates the clutch mechanism, disengaging the clutch by releasing the pressure on the clutch disc from the flywheel. This disconnection allows the driver to shift gears without the transmission being linked to the spinning engine. When the clutch pedal is gradually released, the pressure plate re-engages the clutch disc with the flywheel, reconnecting the engine's power to the transmission and enabling the vehicle to move forward.
